Jharkhand's Rich History

From ancient tribal kingdoms to modern statehood, Jharkhand has a fascinating history.

Prehistoric Era

Early Tribal Settlements

Evidence of human habitation dating back to the Mesolithic period. The region was home to various tribal communities including the Santhal, Munda, and Ho tribes.

5th Century BCE

Magadha Empire

The region became part of the powerful Magadha Empire, with evidence of early iron smelting and metallurgy in the area.

13th-18th Century

Tribal Kingdoms

Various tribal kingdoms flourished, resisting outside domination. The region became known for its rich mineral resources and dense forests.

2000

State Formation

Jharkhand was carved out of Bihar to become India's 28th state on November 15, 2000, fulfilling the long-standing demand of tribal communities.

Rich Tribal Culture

Jharkhand is home to numerous indigenous tribes, each with unique traditions and art forms.

Santhal Tribe

The largest tribal group known for their vibrant dance forms like Chhau and traditional music using indigenous instruments.

Sohrai Art

Traditional mural painting done by tribal women during harvest festivals, depicting nature and animals.

Sarhul Festival

The spring festival celebrated by tribal communities to worship nature and pray for good harvests.

Wildlife Sanctuaries

Jharkhand's forests are home to diverse flora and fauna, with several protected areas.

Betla National Park

Home to tigers, elephants, leopards and various deer species. Offers jungle safaris and elephant rides.

Hazaribagh Wildlife Sanctuary

Spread over 184 sq km, it's known for its rich biodiversity including tigers, leopards and sloth bears.

Dalma Wildlife Sanctuary

Famous for its elephant population and located near Jamshedpur, making it easily accessible.
