Odisha's Glorious History

From ancient kingdoms to colonial rule, Odisha has a fascinating history spanning over 2,000 years.

261 BCE

Kalinga War

The famous war between Emperor Ashoka and the Kalinga kingdom that transformed Ashoka from a conqueror to a Buddhist pacifist. The bloodshed led to his adoption of Buddhism.

1st Century BCE

Golden Age of Kharavela

King Kharavela of the Mahameghavahana dynasty ruled Odisha, known for his military conquests and patronage of Jainism. The Hathigumpha inscriptions document his reign.

7th-13th Century

Temple Architecture Flourishes

The construction of magnificent temples including Lingaraj Temple, Konark Sun Temple, and Jagannath Temple. Odisha's unique Kalinga style of architecture reached its peak.

1936

Formation of Odisha State

Odisha became a separate province on linguistic basis, the first Indian state formed on this principle. Cuttack was the initial capital before moving to Bhubaneswar in 1948.

Odisha's Rich Culture

Odisha boasts a vibrant cultural heritage with unique art forms, festivals, and traditions.

Odissi Dance

One of India's classical dance forms originating from Odisha's temples, characterized by fluid movements and sculpturesque poses.

Pattachitra

Traditional cloth-based scroll painting with mythological narratives, using natural colors and intricate details.

Rath Yatra Festival

The grand chariot festival of Lord Jagannath in Puri, attracting millions of devotees annually since ancient times.

Culinary Delights

Odisha's cuisine is a feast for the senses, blending flavors from land and sea with unique spices and cooking techniques.

Pakhala

Fermented rice dish served with curd, lemon, and fried fish - Odisha's traditional comfort food, especially popular during summers.

Rasagola

Odisha's famous sweet - soft cottage cheese balls soaked in light sugar syrup, with historical roots in the state's temple traditions.

Chhena Poda

Literally "burnt cheese," this caramelized cheesecake-like dessert is Odisha's gift to the culinary world.
