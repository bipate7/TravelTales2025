West Bengal's Glorious Past

A land of intellectuals, revolutionaries, and cultural renaissance, West Bengal has shaped India's history in profound ways.

3rd Century BCE

Mauryan Empire

West Bengal was part of the powerful Mauryan Empire under Emperor Ashoka, with important Buddhist centers at Pundravardhana (modern-day Malda).

4th-6th Century

Gupta Golden Age

The region flourished under the Gupta Empire, with significant developments in art, literature, and science. The terracotta temples of Bishnupur reflect this heritage.

1757

Battle of Plassey

The decisive battle that established British control over Bengal, marking the beginning of colonial rule in India. Calcutta became the capital of British India.

1947

Partition & Independence

Bengal was partitioned along religious lines, with West Bengal becoming part of India and East Bengal (now Bangladesh) joining Pakistan. Millions were displaced in this traumatic event.

Bengali Cuisine

A gastronomic journey through Bengal's rich culinary traditions - from street food to royal feasts.

Traditional Thali

The classic Bengali meal featuring rice, dal, fish curry, vegetable dishes, chutney, and sweets - a perfect balance of flavors.

Kathi Rolls

Kolkata's famous street food - paratha wrapped around kebabs, eggs, and spices, a legacy of the city's Mughlai influences.

Mishti (Sweets)

From rosogolla to sandesh, Bengal's milk-based sweets are world-famous. Don't miss the jaggery-based sweets of Joynagar.

Cultural Treasures

West Bengal's vibrant culture encompasses literature, music, dance, and festivals that captivate visitors.

Durga Puja

The grandest festival of Bengal where the goddess Durga is worshipped in elaborately crafted pandals across the state.

Baul Tradition

The mystic minstrels of Bengal, recognized by UNESCO as an Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.

Rabindra Sangeet

Songs written and composed by Nobel laureate Rabindranath Tagore that form the backbone of Bengali cultural identity.
