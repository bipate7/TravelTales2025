The Soul of East India

Land of red rivers, blue hills, and golden traditions

Where every corner tells a story

From the mighty Himalayas in the north to the serene beaches of the Bay of Bengal, East India is a kaleidoscope of cultures. Home to UNESCO World Heritage Sites like the Sun Temple at Konark, Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, and Kaziranga National Park.

The region pulses with festivals: Durga Puja in Kolkata, Bihu in Assam, Rath Yatra in Puri, and the Hornbill Festival in Nagaland. Its cuisine ranges from the delicate sweets of Bengal to the fiery dishes of the Northeast.

Whether you seek wildlife, spirituality, adventure, or tranquility — East India welcomes you with open arms.

Smart Travel Tips

Everything you need to know before you go

Best Time

October to March for pleasant weather; July‑Sept for lush green Northeast.

Food

Don't miss street food in Kolkata & traditional thalis in Odisha.

Language

English & Hindi widely spoken; local languages add to the charm.

Currency

ATMs in all cities; carry cash for remote areas.
