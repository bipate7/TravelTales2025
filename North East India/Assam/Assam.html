Assam's Glorious Past

A land of ancient kingdoms, warrior tribes, and cultural synthesis, Assam has shaped Northeast India's history in profound ways.

4th Century

Kamarupa Kingdom

Assam was part of the powerful Kamarupa kingdom, with its capital at Pragjyotishpura (modern-day Guwahati). The Kamakhya Temple dates back to this period.

13th Century

Ahom Dynasty

The Ahoms, a Tai group, established a kingdom that ruled Assam for six centuries, successfully resisting Mughal invasions and developing a unique culture.

1826

British Annexation

After the Treaty of Yandabo, Assam came under British rule, becoming part of British India. The tea industry was developed, transforming the region's economy.

1947

Post-Independence

Assam became a state of independent India. The discovery of oil and establishment of refineries marked the beginning of modern industrial development.

Explore Assam

Located in Northeast India, Assam shares international borders with Bhutan and Bangladesh.

Assamese Cuisine

A delightful blend of flavors, Assamese food is simple yet flavorful, with rice as the staple and minimal use of spices.

Traditional Thali

A complete meal with rice, dal, vegetable curries, meat or fish, herbs, and chutneys, served on bell metal plates.

Pitha

Traditional rice cakes prepared during Bihu festivals, with varieties like Til Pitha, Ghila Pitha, and Sunga Pitha.

Assam Tea

World-famous for its robust flavor and bright color, best enjoyed without milk as a morning beverage.

Cultural Heritage

Assam's culture is a vibrant tapestry of diverse ethnic groups, each with unique traditions.

Bihu Festival

The most important cultural festival celebrated three times a year with traditional dance, music, and feasting.

Satras

Vaishnavite monasteries that preserve traditional performing arts like Sattriya dance and Borgeet music.

Traditional Handicrafts

From bamboo products to silk weaving (Muga, Eri, and Pat), Assam has a rich tradition of handicrafts.
