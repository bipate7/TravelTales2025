Cultural Heritage of Manipur

The land of dance, music, and ancient traditions

Land of Classical Arts

Manipur is the birthplace of Ras Lila — the celestial dance of Krishna, and Thang-Ta, the ancient martial art form.

With over 30 indigenous tribes, each community preserves unique festivals, textiles, and oral traditions passed down for centuries.

Sangai Festival

Annual cultural extravaganza (Nov) showcasing Manipuri dance, music, crafts, and cuisine. Named after the endangered brow-antlered deer.

Yaoshang (Holi)

5-day spring festival with street football, folk dances, and community feasts. Unique to Manipur!

Ningol Chakouba

Brother-sister reunion day where married women are invited home for a feast — a beautiful tradition of familial love.

Taste of Manipur

Fresh, fermented, and fiercely flavorful — a cuisine unlike any other

Spicy

Eromba

Fiery chutney of boiled vegetables, fermented fish, and bhut jolokia (ghost pepper). Served with steamed rice.

Sweet

Chak-Hao Kheer

Black rice pudding infused with cardamom and jaggery — a royal dessert with antioxidant-rich indigenous rice.

Fermented

Ngari (Fermented Fish)

The soul of Manipuri cooking — sun-dried fish fermented in earthen pots. Adds umami depth to curries and chutneys.
