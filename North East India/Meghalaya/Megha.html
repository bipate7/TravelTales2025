Adventure Activities

Experience the thrill of Meghalaya's natural playground

Caving Exploration

4-6 hours Small groups

Explore the mysterious caves of Meghalaya including Mawsmai Cave and Krem Liat Prah, one of the longest caves in India.

Living Root Bridge Trek

Full day Moderate difficulty

Trek through lush rainforests to witness the incredible living root bridges, including the famous double-decker bridge in Nongriat.

River Activities

2-4 hours All skill levels

Enjoy kayaking, boating, and swimming in the crystal-clear waters of Umngot River at Dawki and Shnongpdeng.

Festivals & Events

Celebrate the vibrant cultural heritage of Meghalaya

November

Wangala Festival

Also known as the 100 Drums Festival, this Garo harvest celebration features vibrant dances, traditional music, and colorful costumes to thank the Sun God for a bountiful harvest.

April

Shad Suk Mynsiem

A three-day Khasi festival celebrating the joy of life with traditional dances performed by men and women in colorful attire, symbolizing purity and thanksgiving.

July

Behdeinkhlam Festival

Jaintia tribal festival featuring colorful processions, traditional sports, and the beating of roof tops to drive away evil spirits and diseases.

Local Cuisine

Savor the authentic flavors of Meghalaya

Jadoh

Rice dish Pork/Chicken

A flavorful rice dish cooked with meat (usually pork or chicken), spices, and turmeric, often served with potato chips and green chutney.

Dohneiiong

Pork curry Black sesame

A rich pork curry cooked with black sesame seeds, giving it a distinctive nutty flavor and dark color, typically served with rice.

Pumaloi

Rice cake Steamed

A traditional steamed rice cake cooked in special pots, often served during festivals and special occasions with meat or vegetable curries.

Travel Tips

Essential information for your Meghalaya journey

Best Time to Visit

October to May offers pleasant weather for sightseeing. Monsoon (June-September) showcases waterfalls at their best but may limit outdoor activities.

What to Pack

Comfortable walking shoes, rain gear, light woolens for evenings, insect repellent, and a good camera to capture the stunning landscapes.

Getting Around

Hire local taxis or use shared vehicles for inter-city travel. For remote villages, 4WD vehicles are recommended during monsoon season.

Accommodation

Options range from luxury hotels in Shillong to homestays in villages for authentic cultural experiences. Book in advance during peak season.
