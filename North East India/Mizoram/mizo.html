Must-Visit Destinations

Sacred Peak

Phawngpui (Blue Mountain)

Lunglei District 4.9

Mizoram’s highest peak (2,157m). Home to rare orchids, Thlazuang Kham (sacred cliff), and panoramic views of Myanmar hills.

Trekking Guide
Tallest Waterfall

Vantawng Falls

Serchhip District 4.8

India’s 2nd tallest plunge waterfall (228m). Accessible via scenic drive + 1km forest trail. Best after monsoon (Aug–Oct).

Visit Tips

Mizo Cultural Tapestry

Where every dance, song, and craft carries ancestral wisdom.

Khuang (Drum)

Hollowed log drum used in festivals and church ceremonies. Rhythms tell stories of war, harvest, and love.

Puan (Handwoven Cloth)

Vibrant patterns like *Puanchei* (wedding shawl) and *Ngotekherh* (funeral cloth) — each with symbolic meaning.

Cheraw Dance

UNESCO-recognized bamboo dance. Dancers step in/out of clapping bamboo sticks — grace, precision, and rhythm.

Harmonized Choirs

Mizos are famed for 4/6/8-part harmonies in church. Sunday service is a musical pilgrimage for visitors.

Flavors of Mizoram

Bold, smoky, and deeply connected to the land.

Bai

Vegetable stew with *tawtche* (local greens), bamboo shoot, and pork/fish. Slow-cooked in clay pots.

Signature Dish

Vawksa Rep

Smoked pork (*vawksa*) with *rep* (minced pork fat) and *bekang* (fermented soybean). Rich and umami.

Festival Special

Sawhchiar

Savory rice porridge with chicken/pork, ginger, and local herbs. Traditionally eaten for breakfast.

Comfort Food
