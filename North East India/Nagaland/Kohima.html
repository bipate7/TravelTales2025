Kohima - Capital City

4.8 Must Visit Location

Known for its scenic beauty, cultural heritage, and historical significance including the Kohima War Cemetery and State Museum. Kohima offers a unique blend of natural wonders, vibrant tribal culture, and historical landmarks that make it an unforgettable destination.

Explore Kohima Plan Your Trip

Why Visit Kohima?

Scenic Beauty

Breathtaking landscapes with lush green hills, vibrant flora, and picturesque valleys that change with the seasons.

Historical Significance

Home to the famous Kohima War Cemetery, a memorial of World War II's Battle of Kohima, known as the "Stalingrad of the East".

Cultural Heritage

Rich Naga culture with 16 major tribes, traditional festivals, music, dance, and unique handicrafts.

Top Attractions

Kohima War Cemetery

A beautifully maintained memorial with 1,420 Commonwealth burials from WWII. The famous epitaph "When you go home, tell them of us and say, for your tomorrow, we gave our today" is inscribed here.

2-3 hours Central Kohima

Kohima State Museum

Showcases Naga tribal artifacts, traditional costumes, jewelry, weapons, and musical instruments. A perfect place to understand the diverse cultural heritage of Nagaland's tribes.

1-2 hours Bayavu Hill

Dzükou Valley

Known as the "Valley of Flowers", this breathtaking valley is located at 2,452 meters altitude. Famous for its seasonal flowers, rare lilies, and pristine landscape.

Full day 25km from Kohima

Travel Tips

Best Time to Visit

October to May offers pleasant weather. December is ideal for experiencing the famous Hornbill Festival.

Local Cuisine

Try smoked pork with bamboo shoots, axone (fermented soybean), and galho (a rice and vegetable dish).

Souvenirs

Look for traditional Naga shawls, bamboo crafts, wood carvings, and tribal jewelry as memorable souvenirs.

Cultural Festivals

Hornbill Festival

Nagaland's most famous festival held from December 1-10. A vibrant celebration of Naga culture with traditional dances, music, food, and crafts from all tribes.

Dec 1-10 Kisama Heritage Village

Sekrenyi Festival

Celebrated by the Angami tribe in February. A purification festival marked by rituals, feasting, and traditional games that lasts for 10 days.

February Angami Regions

Moatsu Festival

Celebrated by the Ao tribe in May after sowing crops. Features singing, dancing, feasting, and traditional sports over several days.

May Ao Regions

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time to visit Kohima?

The best time to visit Kohima is from October to May when the weather is pleasant. December is particularly popular due to the Hornbill Festival, but it's also the busiest time. For trekking, the post-monsoon months of October and November offer the best conditions.

Do I need a permit to visit Nagaland?

Yes, domestic tourists need an Inner Line Permit (ILP) to visit Nagaland, which can be obtained online or from government offices in Delhi, Kolkata, Guwahati, or Shillong. Foreign tourists need a Restricted Area Permit (RAP), which is typically arranged through tour operators.

What should I pack for a trip to Kohima?

Pack layered clothing as temperatures can vary. Include warm clothes for evenings, comfortable walking shoes, rain gear (especially during monsoon), sunscreen, insect repellent, a hat, and any personal medications. If planning to trek, proper hiking gear is essential.

Is Kohima safe for tourists?

Yes, Kohima is generally safe for tourists. The local people are friendly and welcoming. However, as with any travel destination, it's advisable to take standard precautions, respect local customs, and avoid remote areas after dark without a guide.

Experience Naga Culture

Kohima is the cultural heart of Nagaland, home to diverse Naga tribes each with their unique traditions, festivals, and crafts. The annual Hornbill Festival showcases this rich cultural tapestry with traditional music, dance, food, and handicrafts.

Visit the Kohima Museum to explore the history and heritage of the Naga people, or wander through the local markets to experience the vibrant daily life of this fascinating hill station. Don't miss the opportunity to witness traditional Naga warrior dances and sample local cuisine.
