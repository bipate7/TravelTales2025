Rich Cultural Heritage

Nagaland is home to 16 major tribes and sub-tribes, each with their own distinct customs, traditions, and languages. The state's cultural diversity is reflected in its vibrant festivals, intricate handicrafts, and unique culinary traditions.

The Hornbill Festival, held annually in December, is a spectacular showcase of Naga culture featuring traditional dances, folk songs, indigenous games, and crafts from all the tribes of Nagaland.

Folk Music

Traditional instruments like the bamboo mouth organ and log drums create unique Naga melodies.

Textiles

Intricate handwoven shawls with distinctive tribal patterns are Naga cultural symbols.

Cuisine

Smoked meats, bamboo shoots, and fiery king chili define Naga culinary traditions.

Architecture

Traditional Naga morungs (dormitories) showcase indigenous woodcraft and design.

Major Festivals

Celebrations that showcase Nagaland's vibrant cultural tapestry

1-10 December

Hornbill Festival

The festival of festivals held at Kisama Heritage Village, showcasing all Naga tribes' culture through music, dance, and food.

First Week May

Moatsu Festival

Ao tribe festival marking the end of sowing season with community feasts, songs, and dances.

February

Sekrenyi Festival

Angami tribe's purification festival featuring rituals, feasting, and traditional games.
