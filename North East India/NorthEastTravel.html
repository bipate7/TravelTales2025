About Northeast India

Discover the enchanting beauty and rich cultural heritage of the Seven Sister States — a mosaic of biodiversity, indigenous traditions, and unspoiled landscapes.

The Crown of India

Nestled between the eastern Himalayas and Southeast Asia, Northeast India comprises seven states: Arunachal Pradesh, Assam, Manipur, Meghalaya, Mizoram, Nagaland, and Tripura. Often called the "Paradise Unexplored," this region is home to over 200 ethnic tribes, 220 languages, and unparalleled natural beauty.

From the cloud-kissed peaks of Sikkim-bordering Arunachal to the world’s wettest place in Cherrapunji, and from the mighty Brahmaputra to the living root bridges of Meghalaya — the Northeast is a symphony of nature and culture.

Unmatched Biodiversity

60% of India’s flora & fauna, including rare species like the Hoolock Gibbon and Golden Langur.

Vibrant Cultures

Each state celebrates 10+ indigenous festivals — from Hornbill to Bihu, Sangai to Wangala.

Unique Cuisine

Zero oil, fermented delicacies, bamboo shoots, and fiery bhut jolokia — food that tells a story.

Sustainable Living

Ancient eco-practices like Jhum cultivation and sacred groves reflect deep ecological wisdom.

Celebrations of Life

Festivals here are not just events — they are sacred expressions of identity, harvest, and community.

Hornbill Festival

Dec 1–10

Nagaland’s grand cultural fiesta — all 17 tribes showcase dance, music, crafts, and cuisine in Kisama.

Bihu

Apr 14–15 (Rongali)

Assam’s harvest festival — energetic Bihu dance, dhol, pepa (buffalo horn), and feasting with pitha.

Chapchar Kut

Mar 4–6

Mizoram’s spring festival — bamboo dance (Cheraw), traditional songs, and community feasts after Jhum clearing.

Sangai Festival

Nov 21–30

Manipur’s state festival — celebrates the endangered brow-antlered deer, with classical Ras Lila and boat races.
