About Sikkim

Sikkim, nestled in the Himalayan mountains, is a land of breathtaking beauty and rich cultural heritage. As India's first organic state, it offers pristine landscapes, from snow-capped peaks to lush valleys and sacred lakes.

With its diverse flora and fauna, ancient monasteries, and warm hospitality, Sikkim provides a unique blend of adventure, spirituality, and natural wonders. The state is home to Kangchenjunga, the third highest mountain in the world, and boasts a harmonious mix of Nepalese, Bhutia, and Lepcha cultures.

Whether you're seeking thrilling treks, peaceful meditation retreats, or simply want to immerse yourself in nature's splendor, Sikkim promises an unforgettable experience.

Must-Visit Places

Best: Mar-Oct

Gangtok

Sikkim's capital offers stunning Himalayan views, vibrant markets, and rich cultural experiences. Visit the Rumtek Monastery and enjoy cable car rides with panoramic vistas.

Explore
Best: Mar-Oct

Pelling

Famous for its breathtaking views of Kangchenjunga, Pelling is home to ancient monasteries and waterfalls. The Pemayangtse Monastery is a must-visit spiritual site.

Explore
Best: Mar-Oct

Lachung

A picturesque mountain village known as the gateway to Yumthang Valley. Experience stunning alpine scenery, hot springs, and the Lachung Monastery.

Explore
Best: Apr-Jun

Lachen

Base for the Gurudongmar Lake trek, Lachen offers stunning high-altitude landscapes and a glimpse into traditional Sikkimese village life.

Explore
Best: Mar-May

Yuksom

Historic first capital of Sikkim and starting point for the Dzongri-Goecha La trek. Visit the Dubdi Monastery and the ancient coronation site.

Explore
Best: May-Oct

Nathula Pass

One of the highest motorable roads in the world, this historic Indo-China border offers breathtaking views and a glimpse into military history.
