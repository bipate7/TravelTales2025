About Tripura

Tripura, one of the seven sister states of Northeast India, is a land of stunning natural beauty and rich cultural heritage. Nestled between Bangladesh to the north, south, and west, and the Indian states of Assam and Mizoram to the east, Tripura offers a unique blend of tribal culture, royal history, and picturesque landscapes.

The state is known for its magnificent palaces, ancient temples, lush green valleys, and diverse tribal communities. With over 19 different tribes, each with their own distinct traditions, languages, and festivals, Tripura presents a colorful tapestry of cultures waiting to be explored.

From the royal Ujjayanta Palace in Agartala to the stunning Neermahal water palace, from the sacred Tripura Sundari Temple to the breathtaking views of Jampui Hills, Tripura offers experiences that will leave you spellbound.

Tripura Highlights

Neermahal

The magnificent water palace built by Maharaja Bir Bikram Kishore Manikya in the middle of Rudrasagar Lake, blending Hindu and Islamic architectural styles.

Ujjayanta Palace

The former royal palace of the Manikya kings, now a museum showcasing Tripura's rich history, culture, and artifacts.

Tripura Sundari Temple

One of the 51 Shakti Peethas, this ancient temple is a major pilgrimage site dedicated to Goddess Kali in the form of Tripura Sundari.

Cultural Heritage

Traditional Dances

Tripura's tribal communities have vibrant dance forms like Hojagiri (Reang tribe), Garia (Tripuri tribe), and Lebang Boomani (Chakma tribe), each with unique costumes and rhythms.

Handicrafts

Tripura is famous for its bamboo and cane products, handloom textiles, and traditional jewelry. Each tribe has distinct weaving patterns and craft techniques.

Festivals

Major festivals include Kharchi Puja (July), Garia Puja (April), Ker Puja, and Orange & Tourism Festival in Jampui Hills (November).

Cuisine

Tripuri cuisine features bamboo shoots, fermented fish (Berma), pork, and rice. Must-try dishes include Mui Borok (traditional platter), Chakhwi, and Mosdeng.
