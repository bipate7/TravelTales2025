Detailed Itinerary

Day 1: Arrival in Delhi - City Exploration

Welcome to India! Upon arrival at Delhi International Airport, you'll be greeted by our representative and transferred to your hotel.

After some time to refresh, embark on a guided tour of Old Delhi, visiting:

  • Jama Masjid - India's largest mosque with capacity for 25,000 worshippers
  • Chandni Chowk - The bustling 17th century market area of Old Delhi
  • Raj Ghat - Memorial to Mahatma Gandhi on the banks of the Yamuna River

In the evening, enjoy a welcome dinner featuring Delhi's famous Mughlai cuisine at a renowned restaurant.

Overnight: The Claridges, Delhi | Meals: Dinner

Day 2: Delhi to Agra - The Taj Mahal

After breakfast, drive to Agra (approx. 4 hours). En route, visit Sikandra to see Akbar's Mausoleum, an impressive Mughal architectural masterpiece.

Check into your hotel and after lunch, visit:

  • Taj Mahal - The iconic white marble mausoleum built by Shah Jahan for his wife Mumtaz Mahal (closed Fridays)
  • Agra Fort - The impressive red sandstone fortress that served as the main residence of the Mughal emperors

As the sun sets, enjoy a special viewing of the Taj Mahal from Mehtab Bagh across the Yamuna River, perfectly aligned with the Taj and offering breathtaking views.

Overnight: Trident Agra | Meals: Breakfast, Dinner

Day 3: Agra to Jaipur via Fatehpur Sikri

Early morning optional visit to see the Taj Mahal at sunrise (additional cost) when the marble glows in the soft morning light.

After breakfast, drive to Jaipur (approx. 5 hours), stopping at the abandoned Mughal city of Fatehpur Sikri, a UNESCO World Heritage Site built by Emperor Akbar in 1571 and abandoned just 14 years later due to water shortages.

Arrive in Jaipur and check into your heritage-style hotel. Evening at leisure to explore local markets like Johari Bazaar for jewelry or Bapu Bazaar for textiles.

Overnight: Jai Mahal Palace | Meals: Breakfast, Dinner

Day 4: Jaipur - The Pink City

Full day exploring Jaipur's magnificent sites:

  • Amber Fort - Ride elephants up to the fort entrance (subject to availability) and explore this majestic hilltop fortress with its mirrored halls and stunning views
  • City Palace - The royal residence complex housing museums with an impressive collection of royal costumes and artifacts
  • Jantar Mantar - Ancient astronomical observatory with massive instruments still accurate today
  • Hawa Mahal - The iconic "Palace of Winds" with its honeycombed facade built for royal ladies to observe street life unseen

Evening enjoy a traditional Rajasthani cultural show with dinner at Chokhi Dhani, an ethnic village resort showcasing rural Rajasthani life.

Overnight: Jai Mahal Palace | Meals: Breakfast, Dinner

Day 5: Jaipur to Delhi - Departure

After breakfast, drive back to Delhi (approx. 6 hours).

Depending on your flight schedule, you may have time for some last-minute shopping at Delhi Haat or visits to Qutub Minar and Humayun's Tomb (optional).

Transfer to the airport for your onward journey, taking with you unforgettable memories of India's Golden Triangle.

Meals: Breakfast

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best time of year to take this tour?

The ideal time for the Golden Triangle tour is between October and March when the weather is most pleasant. During these months, daytime temperatures typically range from 20°C to 25°C (68°F to 77°F), making it comfortable for sightseeing. The winter months (December-January) can be cooler in the mornings and evenings, so packing a light jacket is recommended.

Summers (April-June) can be extremely hot, with temperatures often exceeding 40°C (104°F), which can make extensive sightseeing uncomfortable. The monsoon season (July-September) brings heavy rains that may affect travel plans and visibility at some sites, though the landscape becomes beautifully lush during this period.

What should I pack for this tour?

We recommend packing light, comfortable clothing suitable for warm weather, along with these essentials:

  • Clothing: Modest clothing for temple visits (shoulders and knees covered), comfortable walking shoes, light jacket for cooler evenings
  • Sun protection: Hat, sunglasses, sunscreen (SPF 30+ recommended)
  • Health items: Basic medications, hand sanitizer, insect repellent
  • Electronics: Universal power adapter (India uses 230V, Type D sockets), camera with extra memory cards
  • Documents: Passport & copies, travel insurance details, emergency contacts
  • Miscellaneous: Reusable water bottle (we provide purified water), small daypack for excursions
Is this tour suitable for children and elderly travelers?

Yes, our Golden Triangle tour is family-friendly and can be enjoyed by travelers of all ages. For families with children, we offer:

  • Special family packages with connecting rooms
  • Child-friendly guides who can adapt explanations for younger travelers
  • Flexible pacing with adequate rest times built into the itinerary
  • Options to modify activities based on children's interests and energy levels

For elderly travelers, we can arrange:

  • Additional assistance at monuments with many steps
  • More leisurely pacing with extra breaks
  • Special dietary requirements
  • Vehicles with easier access
  • Options to skip certain activities if needed

We recommend discussing any special requirements when booking so we can make appropriate arrangements.

  • Accommodations: Partnering with hotels that follow strict hygiene protocols, contactless check-in where available
  • Group sizes: Small group sizes with appropriate distancing during sightseeing
  • Guides & staff: All trained in COVID-19 safety protocols, daily health checks
  • Flexible policies: Free date changes if you test positive before travel, 24/7 emergency support

We monitor local guidelines and adjust our protocols accordingly to ensure a safe travel experience. Current requirements will be communicated before your departure.
