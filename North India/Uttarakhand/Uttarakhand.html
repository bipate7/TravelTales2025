About Uttarakhand

Known as the "Land of the Gods," Uttarakhand is a paradise for nature lovers and adventure seekers alike

The Divine Himalayan State

Uttarakhand, formerly known as Uttaranchal, is a state in northern India crossed by the Himalayas. The state is known for its natural beauty, with snow-capped peaks, dense forests, winding rivers, and spiritual centers that attract pilgrims from around the world.

From the sacred Ganges in Haridwar to the skiing slopes of Auli, from the wildlife in Jim Corbett National Park to the yoga capital Rishikesh, Uttarakhand offers diverse experiences that cater to every type of traveler.

Learn More

Why Choose Uttarakhand?

Discover what makes Uttarakhand a unique and unforgettable destination

Majestic Himalayas

Home to some of the highest peaks in India, including Nanda Devi and Trishul, offering breathtaking views and trekking opportunities.

Sacred Rivers

The source of the Ganges and Yamuna rivers, with spiritual significance and opportunities for white-water rafting.

Spiritual Heaven

Four sacred Hindu shrines (Char Dham) and numerous ashrams make it India's spiritual capital.

Adventure Sports

From skiing in Auli to rafting in Rishikesh, Uttarakhand offers thrilling adventures for adrenaline junkies.

Wildlife Sanctuaries

Home to Jim Corbett National Park and other reserves with rich biodiversity including tigers, elephants, and rare birds.

Local Cuisine

Unique Garhwali and Kumaoni cuisine with dishes like Kafuli, Bhatt ki Churdkani, and Bal Mithai sweets.

Popular Destinations

Explore the most beautiful places in Uttarakhand that you must visit

Nainital

March-June 300km from Delhi

The charming lake district of India, known for its emerald Naini Lake surrounded by mountains, colonial architecture, and pleasant climate.

  • Boating on Naini Lake
  • Visit to Snow View Point
  • Explore Tiffin Top
  • Naina Devi Temple
Explore More

Rishikesh

Sept-April 240km from Delhi

The yoga capital of the world, where the Ganges emerges from the Himalayas, offering spiritual experiences and thrilling adventures.

  • White-water rafting
  • Laxman Jhula suspension bridge
  • Evening Ganga Aarti
  • Yoga and meditation retreats
Explore More

Auli

Dec-March 500km from Delhi

India's premier skiing destination with panoramic views of Himalayan peaks like Nanda Devi, Trishul, and Dron Parvat.

  • Skiing and snowboarding
  • Auli Ropeway (longest in Asia)
  • Gurso Bugyal alpine meadow
  • Joshimath temple visit
Explore More

Mussoorie

April-June 290km from Delhi

Known as the "Queen of Hills", Mussoorie offers breathtaking views of the Himalayas, colonial charm, and a cool climate ideal for a summer retreat.

  • Walk along Camel’s Back Road
  • Scenic views from Gun Hill
  • Picnic at Kempty Falls
  • Shopping on Mall Road
Explore More

Dehradun

March-June 250km from Delhi

The capital city of Uttarakhand, nestled in the Doon Valley, Dehradun offers a mix of nature, education, and culture with serene landscapes and historical sites.

  • Robber's Cave adventure
  • Sahastradhara springs
  • Forest Research Institute visit
  • Tapkeshwar Mahadev Temple
Explore More

Haridwar

October-April 220km from Delhi

One of the holiest cities in India, Haridwar is famous for its spiritual energy, sacred ghats on the Ganges, and the grand Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri.

  • Evening Ganga Aarti
  • Har Ki Pauri bathing ghat
  • Mansa Devi Temple trek
  • Bharat Mata Mandir visit
Explore More
