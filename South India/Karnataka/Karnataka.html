Cultural Treasures

Karnataka's rich cultural heritage spans centuries, reflected in its art, architecture, traditions, and lifestyle. Discover what makes Karnataka truly unique.

Architectural Marvels

From the intricate carvings of Belur and Halebid to the Indo-Saracenic style of Mysore Palace, Karnataka's architecture tells stories of its glorious past.

Classical Arts

The birthplace of Carnatic music composers like Purandara Dasa, Karnataka has a vibrant tradition of classical music, dance, and folk arts.

Culinary Delights

From Bisi Bele Bath to Mysore Pak, Karnataka's cuisine offers a delicious blend of flavors that vary by region, each with its own specialties.

Traditional Crafts

Explore Karnataka's handicrafts like Mysore silk, sandalwood carvings, Bidriware, and Kinnal paintings that showcase exquisite craftsmanship.

Literary Heritage

With 8 Jnanpith award winners, Kannada literature has a rich tradition dating back to the 9th century with the Kavirajamarga.

Spiritual Diversity

From ancient Hindu temples to Buddhist centers, Jain basadis to churches and mosques, Karnataka is a tapestry of spiritual traditions.

Vibrant Festivals

Karnataka's festivals are a colorful celebration of its traditions, seasons, and spiritual heritage. Plan your visit around these spectacular events.

Mysuru Dasara

October

The 10-day festival culminates in a grand procession with caparisoned elephants, cultural performances, and illuminated palaces in Mysuru.

Hampi Festival

November

A vibrant celebration of art, culture, and heritage at the UNESCO World Heritage site with dance, music, and puppet shows.

Karaga Festival

April

Bangalore's oldest festival dedicated to Draupadi, featuring a spectacular procession with the karaga (water pot) carried by a priest.

Kambala

November-March

Traditional buffalo races in muddy fields, a unique rural sport of coastal Karnataka that attracts thousands of spectators.

Makar Sankranti

January

Celebrated as Suggi in Karnataka, marked by kite flying, bonfires, and the exchange of ellu bella (sesame-jaggery mixture).

Ganesh Chaturthi

August/September

Celebrated with great fervor, especially in coastal regions, with elaborate pandals and immersion processions.
