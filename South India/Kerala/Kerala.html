Unique Kerala Experiences

Discover what makes Kerala truly special through these authentic experiences that showcase the state's culture, traditions, and natural beauty.

Houseboat Stay

Spend a night on traditional Kettuvallam houseboats gliding through palm-fringed canals and lakes of the backwaters.

Ayurvedic Rejuvenation

Experience authentic Ayurvedic treatments and wellness therapies at traditional centers across Kerala.

Kerala Sadya

Savor the traditional vegetarian feast served on banana leaf with over 20 dishes during festivals and special occasions.

Kathakali Performance

Witness this classical dance-drama with elaborate costumes and makeup depicting stories from Hindu epics.

Tea Plantation Walk

Stroll through rolling tea gardens in Munnar, learn about tea processing, and enjoy fresh brews with mountain views.

Elephant Sanctuary Visit

Interact with rescued elephants at ethical sanctuaries and learn about Kerala's special bond with these majestic animals.

Vibrant Festivals

Kerala's festivals are a colorful celebration of its traditions, seasons, and spiritual heritage. Plan your visit around these spectacular events.

Onam

August-September

Kerala's biggest festival marking King Mahabali's return, celebrated with floral decorations, boat races, cultural performances, and the grand Onam Sadya feast.

Nehru Trophy Boat Race

August

Spectacular snake boat race held in Alleppey's Punnamada Lake, featuring 100-foot-long traditional boats with over 100 rowers competing fiercely.

Thrissur Pooram

April-May

Kerala's most spectacular temple festival with decorated elephants, percussion ensembles, and fireworks at Vadakkunnathan Temple in Thrissur.

Vishu

April

Malayali New Year celebrated with Vishukkani (auspicious sight), fireworks, feasting, and giving of Vishukkaineetam (gifts).

Cochin Carnival

December-January

Month-long festival in Fort Kochi featuring street performances, beach celebrations, and the grand procession on New Year's Day.

Attukal Pongala

February-March

World's largest gathering of women for a religious offering, where millions prepare Pongala (rice-based offering) simultaneously in Thiruvananthapuram.
