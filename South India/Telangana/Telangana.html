About Telangana

Discover the vibrant culture and rich heritage of India's youngest state

The Land of Nizams and Heritage

Telangana, formed on June 2, 2014, is the 29th state of India. Located in the southern part of the country, it's known for its rich history, vibrant culture, and rapid development.

The state is home to the historic city of Hyderabad, known as the "City of Pearls" and "City of Nizams." Telangana's unique identity is shaped by its distinct language, traditions, and festivals that reflect its glorious past and promising future.

From the iconic Charminar to the high-tech HITEC City, Telangana beautifully blends tradition with modernity, making it one of India's most dynamic states.

The Rich History of Telangana

Telangana, the youngest state in India, has a history that dates back to the ancient Satavahana dynasty (230 BCE to 220 CE). The region has been ruled by various dynasties including the Kakatiyas, the Qutb Shahis, and the Nizams, each leaving behind a unique cultural and architectural legacy.

Satavahana Era (230 BCE - 220 CE)

The Satavahanas established their kingdom with Pratishthana (modern Paithan) as their capital, contributing to the development of art, architecture, and trade.

Kakatiya Dynasty (1083–1323 CE)

The Kakatiyas built the iconic Warangal Fort and the Thousand Pillar Temple, establishing Telangana as a center of art and culture.

Qutb Shahi Period (1518–1687)

The Qutb Shahi dynasty founded Hyderabad and built architectural marvels like the Charminar and Golconda Fort.

Asaf Jahi Dynasty (1724–1948)

The Nizams ruled Hyderabad State, making it one of the richest princely states in India, known for its pearls, diamonds, and the famous Koh-i-Noor diamond.

Formation of Telangana State (2014)

After a long struggle, Telangana was officially formed as the 29th state of India on June 2, 2014, with Hyderabad as its capital.

Vibrant Festivals of Telangana

Experience the colorful celebrations that define Telangana's cultural identity

Bathukamma

September-October

A vibrant floral festival celebrated by women, where colorful flowers are arranged in concentric circles to form Bathukamma.

Bonalu

July-August

A Hindu festival where offerings are made to the goddess Mahakali, featuring colorful processions and traditional music.

Sammakka Saralamma Jatara

Once in 2 years (February)

One of the largest tribal religious gatherings in the world, attracting millions of devotees to Medaram.

Telangana Cuisine

Savor the authentic flavors of Telangana's culinary heritage

Hyderabadi Biryani

Main Course

The world-famous aromatic rice dish cooked with meat, spices, and saffron, a legacy of the Nizams.

Baghara Baingan

Side Dish

Baby brinjals cooked in a rich, spicy peanut and sesame paste, a traditional Hyderabadi delicacy.

Sarva Pindi

Snack

A savory rice flour pancake mixed with peanuts, chilies, and spices, a traditional Telangana breakfast.

Wildlife & Nature

Explore the natural treasures and biodiversity of Telangana

Kawal Wildlife Sanctuary

Adilabad District

Home to tigers, leopards, and various deer species, this sanctuary is a biodiversity hotspot in Telangana.

Nagarjuna Sagar

Nalgonda District

One of the largest man-made lakes in the world, surrounded by the Nagarjuna Sagar Wildlife Sanctuary.

Pranahita Wildlife Sanctuary

Adilabad District

Known for its diverse flora and fauna, including sloth bears, panthers, and various bird species.
