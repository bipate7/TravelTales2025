New Coral Conservation Initiative

The Andaman administration has launched a new initiative allowing tourists to participate in coral reef restoration activities while enjoying the pristine beaches and crystal-clear waters of these tropical islands.

This innovative program, developed in collaboration with marine biologists and environmental organizations, enables visitors to contribute directly to the conservation of the fragile marine ecosystem. Participants receive training on coral transplantation techniques and get to work alongside experts in designated conservation zones.

The Andaman Islands are home to some of the most biodiverse coral reefs in the world, but they face threats from climate change, pollution, and unsustainable tourism practices. This program aims to combat these challenges while raising awareness about marine conservation.

How to Participate

Join our conservation program and make a tangible difference in preserving the Andaman Islands' marine ecosystems. Our programs are designed for both tourists and serious conservation enthusiasts.

  • Choose from half-day, full-day, or multi-day conservation experiences
  • No prior experience required - full training provided
  • Suitable for adults and children above 12 years
  • All equipment provided including snorkeling gear
  • Small group sizes for personalized attention
  • Flexible scheduling to fit your travel plans

Tour operators across the islands have been trained to implement the program responsibly, ensuring minimal impact on the marine environment. The response from tourists has been overwhelmingly positive, with many describing it as the most meaningful part of their vacation.
