Indira Gandhi Memorial Tulip Garden

A Floral Paradise in Srinagar

The Indira Gandhi Memorial Tulip Garden in Srinagar has opened its doors to visitors with over 1.5 million vibrant tulips in full bloom. This year, the garden showcases 68 stunning varieties, offering a breathtaking spectacle of colors against the backdrop of the majestic Zabarwan Mountains.

Spread over 30 hectares, the garden is Asia's largest tulip garden and has become a major tourist attraction since its establishment in 2007. The tulip festival, which runs from late March to early April, attracts thousands of visitors from across India and around the world.

This year, the garden authorities have introduced several new features including guided tours, photography workshops, and cultural performances that showcase the rich heritage of Kashmir.

Plan Your Visit

Best Time to Visit

The tulip festival runs from late March to early April when the flowers are in full bloom. Morning visits are recommended for the best experience.

Ticket Information

Tickets can be purchased online or at the entrance. Special rates are available for students, seniors, and groups.

Location

Located in the Zabarwan foothills, overlooking the famous Dal Lake in Srinagar, Kashmir.

Photography

Photography is encouraged throughout the garden. Professional photography sessions require prior permission.
