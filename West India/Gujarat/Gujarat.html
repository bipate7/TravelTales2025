Welcome to Gujarat

A land of diverse landscapes, rich heritage, and vibrant culture that will captivate your heart

The Jewel of Western India

Gujarat, located on India's western coast, is a state that beautifully blends tradition with modernity. Known as the land of Mahatma Gandhi and Sardar Patel, Gujarat offers a unique travel experience with its colorful festivals, exquisite handicrafts, and architectural marvels.

From the Asiatic lions of Gir to the salt marshes of the Rann of Kutch, from the ancient stepwells to the modern skyline of Ahmedabad, Gujarat presents a fascinating tapestry of experiences that will leave you spellbound.

The state's rich cultural heritage is reflected in its music, dance, arts, and cuisine. Gujarati hospitality is legendary, and visitors are often treated like honored guests.

Gujarati Culture & Heritage

Experience the vibrant traditions of Gujarat

Cuisine
Heritage
Festivals

Gujarati Thali

A complete meal featuring dal, kadhi, shaak (vegetables), roti, rice, and sweets. Known for its subtle sweetness and variety.

Traditional meal across Gujarat

Dhokla

Steamed savory cake made from fermented rice and chickpea batter, typically served with green chutney. A popular breakfast and snack item.

Originated in Gujarat

Thepla

Soft flatbread made from wheat flour, gram flour, and fenugreek leaves. Often eaten during travels and as breakfast with pickles or yogurt.

Gujarati specialty
UNESCO Site

Rani ki Vav

An intricately constructed stepwell in Patan, built in the 11th century. Known for its exquisite sculptures depicting Hindu deities and mythological scenes.

UNESCO Site

Champaner-Pavagadh

A historical park with remains of a 16th century capital, featuring forts, palaces, mosques, and temples showcasing the blend of Hindu-Muslim architecture.

Architectural Marvel

Modhera Sun Temple

An 11th century temple dedicated to the Sun God, known for its intricate carvings and the unique architectural alignment with the equinoxes.

Rann Utsav

A 3-month long festival celebrating the cultural heritage of Kutch, featuring folk dances, music performances, handicraft exhibitions, and full moon viewing in the white desert.

November - February

Navratri

Gujarat's most vibrant festival featuring 9 nights of Garba and Dandiya Raas dances. People dress in colorful traditional attire and dance to devotional songs.

September/October

International Kite Festival

Held during Makar Sankranti, the skies of Ahmedabad fill with colorful kites from around the world. Includes kite flying competitions and night flying with illuminated kites.

Gujarat Travel Map

Plan your journey across Gujarat's diverse regions

Key Regions to Explore

  • Kutch: White desert, handicrafts, Rann Utsav
  • Saurashtra: Gir National Park, Somnath Temple, Dwarka
  • Central Gujarat: Ahmedabad, Vadodara, Champaner
  • South Gujarat: Surat, Saputara, Dang forests
  • North Gujarat: Patan, Modhera, Polo Forest

Travel Tips

  • Best time to visit: October to March (pleasant weather)
  • Summers (April-June) can be extremely hot, except in hill stations
  • Monsoon (July-September) brings lush greenery but some areas may flood
  • Rann Utsav runs from November to February
  • Book Gir safari permits 3-6 months in advance
