Welcome to Madhya Pradesh

Madhya Pradesh, literally meaning "Central Province", is located in the geographic heart of India. The state is home to some of the country's most spectacular monuments, three UNESCO World Heritage Sites, numerous wildlife sanctuaries, and sacred pilgrimage sites.

From the erotic sculptures of Khajuraho to the Buddhist stupas of Sanchi, from the royal heritage of Gwalior to the spiritual town of Ujjain, Madhya Pradesh offers an incredible diversity of experiences for every kind of traveler.

The state is also home to some of India's finest tiger reserves including Bandhavgarh, Kanha, and Pench - offering some of the best wildlife experiences in the country.

Why Visit Madhya Pradesh?

Discover what makes Madhya Pradesh special

Rich Heritage

Home to three UNESCO World Heritage Sites: Khajuraho Group of Monuments, Buddhist Monuments at Sanchi, and the Rock Shelters of Bhimbetka.

Wildlife Paradise

Madhya Pradesh is known as the "Tiger State of India" with the highest population of tigers in the country across its numerous national parks.

Vibrant Culture

Experience diverse tribal cultures, classical dance forms, traditional crafts, and colorful festivals throughout the year.

Gond Tribal Art

Vibrant folk art characterized by intricate patterns and bright colors, depicting flora, fauna and tribal life. A unique visual language of the Gond tribe.

Poha-Jalebi

MP's signature breakfast - flattened rice cooked with spices, peanuts and served with crispy jalebis. A perfect sweet-savory combination.

Khajuraho Dance Festival

Annual festival showcasing classical dance forms against the backdrop of Khajuraho temples. A celebration of India's rich performing arts heritage.
